It can sit beside ERP and storefront systems while keeping warehouse execution in one platform.

Core Platform

Designed for real warehouse work, not just dashboards or generic ERP screens.

Inventory control with traceability

Manage stock by company, BIN, SKU, lot number, and expiration date so controlled products stay safe inside the same warehouse.

  • Lot and expiry tracking per item
  • Multiple lots for the same SKU
  • Customer inventory exports

Customer portal without cross-account leakage

Each customer sees only their own inventory, orders, and inbounds while your warehouse team stays in the full operational workspace.

  • Inventory and order visibility
  • Inbound and order submission
  • Company-scoped access rules

Warehouse execution from release to ship

Move released orders into picking, staging, shipping, and confirmation with the same company-safe controls used across the platform.

  • Draft, released, picked, staged, shipped
  • Operational documents
  • Customer-facing shipment updates

FEFO allocation for expiry-sensitive stock

When inventory is expiry-controlled, released orders can allocate by earliest usable expiration so the right stock gets picked first.

  • FEFO allocation logic
  • Lot-level mobile pick guidance
  • Safer shipment consumption

Integrations for ERP, storefront, and fulfillment lanes

Pull work from storefronts or SFTP folders and push confirmations back out on a schedule that matches customer requirements across warehouse and ERP-connected workflows.

  • Shopify sync
  • SFTP import and export lanes
  • Per-company scheduling

Commercial packaging for one WMS platform

Super users can enable modules by company so you can launch standard, premium, or industry-specific warehouse software plans without separate codebases.

  • Portal access control
  • Billing and integrations control
  • Scalable platform rollout

Implementation & Support

Roll out the platform in stages instead of exposing every workflow on day one.

WMS365 is built for phased implementation. That means you can launch inventory and outbound first, then layer on customer portal access, billing, and integrations as each company is ready.

  • Company-by-company rollout instead of a one-time cutover
  • Operational setup around items, locations, lots, and customer access
  • Guided expansion into Shopify, SFTP, and customer-facing workflows
